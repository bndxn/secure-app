# Half Marathon Training Plan - Berlin Half Marathon (29 March 2026)

## Goal
Half marathon on 29 March 2026, following Hal Higdon's Intermediate 2 plan.

## Key Pace Targets
- **Race Pace**: 4:30 min/km (target half marathon pace)
- **Easy/Long Run Pace**: 5:00-6:00 min/km (30-90 seconds slower than race pace)
- **Tempo Pace**: Near 10K race pace (comfortably hard, ~4:45-5:00 min/km)
- **Interval Pace (5K pace)**: ~3:45-4:00 min/km (for 400m repeats)
- **HR Zones**: Zone 2 for easy runs, Zone 3-4 for tempo, Zone 4-5 for intervals

## Workout Types
- **Long Runs**: Progressive distance, easy pace (5:00-6:00 min/km), key for endurance
- **Tempo Runs**: 30-45 min continuous with buildup in middle to near 10K pace (~4:45-5:00 min/km)
- **Intervals**: 400m repeats at 5K pace (~3:45-4:00 min/km) with jog/walk recovery
- **Pace Runs**: Run at race pace (4:30 min/km) to get used to race pace
- **Easy Runs**: Recovery runs at easy pace (5:00-6:00 min/km)
- **Cross-Training**: Aerobic activity (cycling, swimming, etc.) on Mondays
- **Rest**: Critical for recovery, especially before/after long runs

**Key Principles:**
- Long runs should feel like "a stroll in the park" - don't push the pace
- Tempo runs: 10-15 min easy, 15-20 min at tempo pace, 5-10 min easy
- Intervals: Walk or jog recovery between 400m repeats
- Rest is as important as runs - be realistic about fatigue
- Can juggle workouts day-to-day if needed, but maintain consistency
